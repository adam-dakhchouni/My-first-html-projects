Ibn Battuta -Summary 🧕

      Full Name: Abu Abdullah Muhammad Ibn Battuta

      Born: February 25, 1304, in Tangier, Morocco

      Died: 1368 or 1369 (uncertain)
Who was he? 🧭

      Ibn Battuta was a famous Moroccan explorer and traveler. He is best known for traveling more than 75,000 miles

      (over 120,000 kilometers) across Africa, the Middle East, India, and parts of Asia during the 14th century.

      His journeys lasted about 30 years!
His Travels 🌍

      He started his journey in 1325, originally to go on a pilgrimage (Hajj) to Mecca. But after that, he kept traveling for decades, visiting places like:

      Egypt and the Middle East

      Persia and East Africa

      India and the Maldives

      China and Central Asia

      Andalusia (Spain) and Mali (West Africa)
His Book 📖

      After returning home, he told his story to a scholar who wrote it down in a book called:

      "Rihla" (The Journey)

      It became one of the most important travel books in history, full of information about the places and people of that time.
Legacy 🌟

      Ibn Battuta is considered one of the greatest travelers of all time. Many schools, airports, and roads around the world are named after him.
